KaleidoSwap · USDT on RGB · Decision Brief

Bridging L-USDt to RGB on Bitcoin: every option, one verdict

There is no USDT on RGB today. The only Tether near Bitcoin is L-USDt, a native Liquid asset — so any USDT on RGB must be created from it. Each option below is a different answer to one question: who mints, and who enforces the backing? All of them share the same irreducible base trust: Tether's solvency and the Liquid federation.

The options

P SPV peg-in mint audit-grade now · the pick

Liquid's peg-in pattern, ported to RGB. Anyone locks L-USDt in a claim-tagged deposit on Liquid and self-mints on Bitcoin RGB with the SPV proof; every holder's validation enforces the rules forever.

Pro
  • No trusted minter; inflation impossible by rule
  • Right chain: dollars reach Bitcoin + Lightning RGB
  • Deposits theft-proof (tag) and double-claim-proof (gate serialization)
  • Refund covenant: censorship never traps funds
  • Buildable now from proven spike pieces
Con
  • Delay-only coordinator on the Bitcoin gate (v1)
  • Peg-out gated, not trustless (same as Liquid's own peg)
  • Audit-grade until the AluVM introspection RFC lands
  • Heavier validation; no light-client story yet

A Issuer vault ships now · trusted

KaleidoSwap mints on Bitcoin RGB against a published L-USDt vault on Liquid; anyone audits vault ≥ supply with no oracle. Same topology as P — only who may mint differs.

Pro
  • Shippable immediately; engineering is weeks
  • Best-in-class transparency for a wrapped asset
  • Feeds the maker; showcases stable LN channels
Con
  • Trusted minter: fraud window, custody blast radius
  • Issuer-of-record regulation (MiCA EMT) — the long pole
  • Dead vault capital; stablecoin cold start

B Covenant mint on Liquid needs RFC #12

The RGB asset lives on Liquid; one transaction mints and locks backing, and a Simplicity covenant makes the chain reject unbacked mints. Proven end to end in the spike.

Pro
  • Chain-enforced backing; permissionless minting
  • Programmable dollars (gates, staking, vaults)
Con
  • Inflation gap: chain can't read RGB amounts (audit-killed only)
  • Wrong chain: competes with L-USDt itself, misses Lightning
  • Immutable covenant = unpatchable if buggy

B⁺ B + AluVM introspection two RFCs away

Validators read the witness transaction: an unbacked mint becomes invalid for everyone; tranche-bound redemption yields a two-sided arbitrage peg. The endgame for programmable dollars on Liquid.

Pro
  • No minter, no inflation, mechanically defended peg
  • Doubly enforced: covenant + client-side validation
Con
  • RFC #12 and a second consensus change — years, not ours to control
  • Still Liquid-hosted; formal verification mandatory

C No wrap — swaps at the edge live today

Hold native L-USDt; atomic-swap at the boundary whenever value enters RGB or Lightning rails. This is the existing maker model — and the safety net under every other option.

Pro
  • Zero new trust, zero issuance risk, already works
Con
  • No dollars inside RGB/LN — no stable channels
  • Spread on every crossing; needs maker liquidity

D Tether issues natively their call

Canonical USDT on RGB from the issuer itself. Not a plan — an exit condition: every wrap above is designed to retire gracefully into it via 1:1 maker swaps.

Pro
  • No wrapper trust at all; unlimited size
Con
  • External timing; they may pick other rails or none

E Federated issuer still trusted

Option A with an N-of-M consortium holding the vault and co-signing mints. A maturation knob for A as float grows — not a distinct launch path.

Pro
  • No single-key custody failure
Con
  • Still a trusted minter, plus governance and quorum liveness

F Synthetic dollar excluded

No Tether: USD exposure manufactured from BTC via perp hedging or DLCs. Different product, soft peg at the tails, hedge-venue or oracle trust. Out by decision.

Verdict

P — the SPV peg-in mint

Port Liquid's peg-in to RGB: the depositor is the minter, and every holder's own validation — not any issuer — enforces that no mint is unbacked, stolen, or double-claimed. Dollars land on the chain where the demand actually is: Bitcoin and Lightning RGB rails.

1 · LOCK

Tagged deposit on Liquid

L-USDt into a covenant tranche (your key, ~72h refund branch) + an OP_RETURN committing to your RGB claim. The tag welds the deposit to you alone.

2 · MINT

Self-mint on Bitcoin RGB

A mint transition carrying the claim package: deposit tx, Merkle path, federation-signed headers, the tag's opening. ~4 KB, off-chain, verified in milliseconds.

3 · VALIDATE

Everyone, forever

Nine rules per mint: real deposit, right vault and asset, amount ≥ minted, tag opens to this seal, never claimed before, supply arithmetic. Fail one → invalid for every honest wallet.

Trust, stated precisely: Tether + Liquid federation (unavoidable for any USDT), a delay-only coordinator on the Bitcoin gate whose censorship is capped by the refund covenant, and — until the AluVM introspection RFC upgrades the rules from wallet-policy to validation itself — conforming wallets. Exits: maker atomic swaps for everyone, instantly; gated wholesale peg-out for size, exactly like Liquid's own peg.

C · live now→ P · audit-grade bridge→ AluVM RFC · validation-grade→ B⁺ · programmable dollars→ D · Tether arrives, wrap retires 1:1

Status

The evidence base is built and CI-enforced: RGB anchors on Liquid, four Simplicity covenants, the backing-aware BFA schema with full-history audit (a chain-confirmed over-mint caught, a falsified history rejected), and round-trip atomic swaps between Bitcoin and Liquid RGB assets. P's design is complete (schema, tag format, nine validation rules, refund covenant, threat model, four-phase build plan) — each phase ~80% assembled from proven pieces. Upstream, the maintainers responded favorably to our multi-chain RFC and asked us to wait for their consensus refactor before submitting the patch.
